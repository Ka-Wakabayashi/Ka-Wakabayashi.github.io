Theoretical condensed matter physics

Katsunori Wakabayashi

Group Leader, Quantum Materials Design Group
National Institute for Materials Science (NIMS)

I am a theoretical physicist working on quantum materials, two-dimensional systems, and topological phenomena. My research focuses on electronic, optical, and transport properties of low-dimensional materials, including graphene, transition-metal dichalcogenides, moiré systems, and topological photonic structures.

Research

Quantum transport in 2D materials

Theory of electronic, valley, spin, and optical transport in graphene, transition-metal dichalcogenides, and related low-dimensional systems.

Moiré exciton photoluminescence

Inverse analysis of hyperspectral photoluminescence maps, disorder metrology, and exciton physics in moiré heterostructures.

Topological photonics and lattice models

Topological states, corner and hinge modes, and photonic analogues of condensed-matter systems.

Quantum-information-inspired materials physics

Exploring connections between lattice physics, topology, stabilizer-like structures, and quantum information concepts.
